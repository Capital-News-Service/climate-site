Lung Disease

  • Our lungs are continually communicating with and always in contact with the environment. When cold air enters our lungs, it reduces our body's natural response mechanisms of the upper respiratory tract.

  • Cold air can also suppress immune responses to infections like influenza and the common cold.

  • When our immune system is hindered, you are much more likely to contract a lung disease, therefore, during prolonged spells of cold and dry air our bodies are more susceptible to bronchospasm, asthma, and exacerbators of COPD.
